RTX Corporation

Company Overview

RTX Corporation (formerly Raytheon Technologies) is an American multinational aerospace and defense conglomerate headquartered in Arlington, Virginia. It is the world's largest aerospace and defense company by revenue, with over 185,000 employees globally.

Founding & History

1922

Company Founded

Raytheon was founded in Cambridge, Massachusetts by Laurence K. Marshall, Vannevar Bush, and Charles G. Smith as the American Appliance Company. The company initially focused on refrigeration technology before shifting to electronics.

1925

Name Change & Growth

The company changed its name to Raytheon Manufacturing Company and began marketing its rectifier tubes, achieving commercial success in the radio industry.

1940s

World War II Era

During WWII, Raytheon mass-manufactured magnetron tubes for microwave radar systems. By 1945, the company was responsible for approximately 80% of all magnetrons manufactured, establishing itself as a defense contractor.

1945-1947

Microwave Oven Invention

Raytheon engineer Percy Spencer accidentally discovered that magnetrons could heat food, leading to the invention of the microwave oven. The company demonstrated the "Radarange" for commercial use in 1947.

1950s

Missile Development

Raytheon developed the first guidance system for a missile that could intercept a flying target. The Lark missile became the first weapon to destroy a target aircraft in flight (1950).

2020

Merger with United Technologies

Raytheon merged with the aerospace subsidiaries of United Technologies Corporation (UTC) to form Raytheon Technologies, creating the world's largest aerospace and defense company.

2023

Renamed to RTX Corporation

The company officially changed its name from Raytheon Technologies to RTX Corporation, consolidating its brand identity.

5 Monumental Company Events

1

WWII Radar Production (1940-1945)

Raytheon's mass production of magnetron tubes for radar systems was crucial to Allied victory. The company produced 80% of all magnetrons, transforming from a small electronics firm into a major defense contractor. This established Raytheon's reputation and set the foundation for its future in defense technology.

2

Microwave Oven Invention (1945)

Percy Spencer's accidental discovery of microwave cooking revolutionized the food industry. The first commercial microwave, the "Radarange," weighed 750 pounds and cost $5,000. This innovation demonstrated Raytheon's ability to develop dual-use technologies for both military and civilian applications.

3

Patriot Missile System Success (1991)

During the Gulf War, Raytheon's Patriot missile system gained worldwide recognition for intercepting Iraqi Scud missiles. This success cemented Raytheon's position as a leader in missile defense technology and led to billions in international contracts.

4

UTC Merger (April 2020)

The $121 billion merger between Raytheon and United Technologies' aerospace divisions created the world's largest aerospace and defense company. This "merger of equals" combined Raytheon's defense systems with UTC's commercial aerospace expertise (Pratt & Whitney engines, Collins Aerospace).

5

$1.7B LTAMDS Contract (2025)

RTX's Raytheon unit was awarded a $1.7 billion contract in September 2025 to deliver the Lower Tier Air and Missile Defense Sensor (LTAMDS) to the U.S. Army and Poland. This next-generation radar system represents the future of air defense technology.

Nokia Corporation

Company Overview

Nokia Corporation is a Finnish multinational telecommunications, information technology, and consumer electronics company headquartered in Espoo, Finland. Founded in 1865, Nokia has transformed from a paper mill to a global leader in 5G network infrastructure.

$20.8B 2024 Revenue
$35.6B Market Cap
160+ Years In Business
#3 Network Equipment

Founding & History

1865

Company Founded

Nokia was founded by Fredrik Idestam on May 12, 1865, as a single paper mill operation in Tampere, Finland. A second pulp mill was opened in 1868 near the town of Nokia, which gave the company its name.

1967

Nokia Corporation Formed

Nokia Ab, Finnish Rubber Works, and Finnish Cable Works merged to create the Nokia Corporation, beginning the company's diversification into electronics and telecommunications.

1981

Mobile Telephony Begins

Nokia's subsidiary Mobira launched the Nordic Mobile Telephone (NMT) service, the world's first international cellular network and the first to allow international roaming.

1992

GSM Revolution

Under CEO Jorma Ollila, Nokia introduced the Nokia 1011, the world's first GSM digital phone. The Finnish Prime Minister made the world's first GSM call using Nokia equipment in 1991.

1998

World's Largest Phone Maker

Nokia became the world's largest mobile phone manufacturer, a position it would hold for 14 consecutive years until 2012.

2014

Microsoft Acquisition

Microsoft acquired Nokia's mobile phone division for $7.2 billion. Nokia pivoted to focus entirely on telecommunications infrastructure and networking equipment.

2016

Alcatel-Lucent Acquisition

Nokia completed its acquisition of Alcatel-Lucent for $15.6 billion, significantly expanding its network equipment capabilities and positioning for 5G leadership.

5 Monumental Company Events

1

First GSM Call (1991)

The world's first GSM call was made by Finnish Prime Minister Harri Holkeri using Nokia equipment. This moment marked the beginning of the modern mobile phone era and established Nokia as a pioneer in cellular technology. GSM became the global standard for mobile communications.

2

Becoming #1 in Mobile Phones (1998)

Nokia surpassed Motorola to become the world's largest mobile phone manufacturer. At its peak in 2007, Nokia controlled 49.4% of the global mobile phone market. This dominance lasted 14 years and made Nokia synonymous with mobile phones worldwide.

3

Microsoft Partnership & Acquisition (2011-2014)

In 2011, Nokia announced a strategic partnership with Microsoft to use Windows Phone. In 2014, Microsoft acquired Nokia's phone division for $7.2 billion. This marked the end of Nokia's mobile phone era but allowed the company to refocus on telecommunications infrastructure.

4

Alcatel-Lucent Acquisition (2016)

Nokia acquired Alcatel-Lucent for $15.6 billion, gaining Bell Labs and significantly expanding its network infrastructure capabilities. This strategic acquisition positioned Nokia as a major player in 5G technology development and network equipment.

5

Nokia 8.3 5G Launch (2020)

Nokia (through HMD Global) announced the Nokia 8.3 5G, marketed as the world's first truly global 5G smartphone. This symbolized Nokia's return to mobile innovation and its leadership in 5G technology on both the infrastructure and device sides.
